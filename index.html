Our Solar System: A Cosmic Neighborhood

The solar system is the collection of eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, comets, asteroids, and other objects that orbit the Sun. It formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing cloud of gas and dust. The Sun, which makes up about 99.86% of the solar system's mass, is at the center, and the planets orbit around it in nearly circular paths.

The Eight Planets

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the solar system. It has no atmosphere and extreme temperature variations, ranging from 430°C during the day to -180°C at night.

Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide. Its surface temperature averages 462°C, making it the hottest planet in the solar system.

Earth

Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has a protective atmosphere, liquid water, and a magnetic field that shields it from harmful solar radiation. Its unique features include continents, oceans, and a dynamic climate.

Mars

Mars, often called the Red Planet, has a thin atmosphere and cold temperatures. It is known for its polar ice caps, canyons, and evidence of past water activity, making it a prime candidate for future human exploration.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, known for its Great Red Spot—a massive storm that has raged for centuries. It has a strong magnetic field and numerous moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its extensive ring system, composed of billions of ice and rock particles. It has a complex weather system and is the second-largest planet in the solar system. Its moons, like Titan, are of significant interest for their potential habitability.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant with a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It rotates on its side, giving it an unusual tilt of 98 degrees. It has faint rings and a cold, windy atmosphere.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and known for its strong winds, reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h. It has a deep blue color due to methane and is an ice giant with a complex weather system.

Dwarf Planets

Pluto

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet, is now classified as a dwarf planet. It orbits the Sun in a highly elliptical path and has a thin atmosphere composed of nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide. Its surface features include glaciers and mountains.

Profile photo not provided

Eris

Eris is a dwarf planet located in the scattered disc, a distant region of the solar system. It is slightly smaller than Pluto and has a highly elliptical orbit. Eris is notable for being the most distant known dwarf planet.

Haumea

Haumea is an elongated, fast-spinning dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt. It has a unique shape due to its rapid rotation and is known for its bright surface, which reflects a significant amount of sunlight.

Makemake

Makemake is another dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, known for its bright surface and high albedo. It has a small moon named S/2005 (136105) 1, discovered during observations of Makemake's occultation.

Moons & Other Satellites

Earth's Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth-largest moon in the solar system. It plays a crucial role in Earth's climate and tides. Its surface is covered with craters and vast plains called maria.

Jupiter's Moons

Jupiter has at least 79 moons, with four large ones—Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto—discovered by Galileo Galilei. Ganymede is the largest moon in the solar system, larger than Mercury.

Saturn's Rings & Moons

Saturn has 146 known moons, with Titan being the largest and the only moon with a significant atmosphere. The rings, composed of ice and rock particles, are a defining feature of Saturn.

Exoplanets: Planets Beyond Our Solar System

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. As of 2023, over 5,000 exoplanets have been discovered using various methods, including the transit method, radial velocity, and direct imaging. These discoveries have expanded our understanding of planetary diversity and the potential for life beyond Earth.

Glossary

Asteroid

A rocky body that orbits the Sun, primarily found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.

Comet

A small body of ice, dust, and rock that orbits the Sun, often developing a glowing coma and tail when near the Sun.

Dwarf Planet

A celestial body that orbits the Sun, is spherical in shape, and has not cleared its orbit of other debris.

Kuiper Belt

A region of space beyond Neptune containing many icy bodies, including dwarf planets like Pluto.

Orbit

The path that a celestial body follows as it revolves around another body, typically the Sun.
